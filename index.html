Engineering Formulas

Essential formulas for mechanical engineering

Mechanics

Stress

σ = F/A

Where: σ = stress, F = force, A = cross-sectional area

Strain

ε = ΔL/L₀

Where: ε = strain, ΔL = deformation, L₀ = original length

Hooke's Law

σ = E·ε

Where: E = Young's modulus

Shear Stress

τ = F/A

Where: τ = shear stress, F = shear force, A = area

Bending Stress

σ = M·y/I

Where: M = bending moment, y = distance from neutral axis, I = moment of inertia

Torsional Stress

τ = T·r/J

Where: T = torque, r = radius, J = polar moment of inertia

Dynamics

Newton's Second Law

F = m·a

Where: F = force, m = mass, a = acceleration

Kinetic Energy

KE = ½·m·v²

Where: KE = kinetic energy, m = mass, v = velocity

Potential Energy

PE = m·g·h

Where: PE = potential energy, g = gravity, h = height

Momentum

p = m·v

Where: p = momentum, m = mass, v = velocity

Angular Momentum

L = I·ω

Where: L = angular momentum, I = moment of inertia, ω = angular velocity

Thermodynamics

First Law of Thermodynamics

ΔU = Q - W

Where: ΔU = change in internal energy, Q = heat added, W = work done

Ideal Gas Law

PV = nRT

Where: P = pressure, V = volume, n = moles, R = gas constant, T = temperature

Heat Transfer (Conduction)

Q = -k·A·(dT/dx)

Where: Q = heat transfer rate, k = thermal conductivity, A = area

Heat Transfer (Convection)

Q = h·A·(Tₛ - T∞)

Where: h = heat transfer coefficient, Tₛ = surface temperature, T∞ = fluid temperature

Carnot Efficiency

η = 1 - (T_c/T_h)

Where: η = efficiency, T_c = cold reservoir temp, T_h = hot reservoir temp

Fluid Mechanics

Bernoulli's Equation

P₁/ρg + v₁²/2g + z₁ = P₂/ρg + v₂²/2g + z₂

Where: P = pressure, ρ = density, v = velocity, z = elevation

Reynolds Number

Re = ρ·v·D/μ

Where: ρ = density, v = velocity, D = diameter, μ = dynamic viscosity

Continuity Equation

A₁·v₁ = A₂·v₂

Where: A = cross-sectional area, v = velocity

Darcy-Weisbach Equation

h_f = f·(L/D)·(v²/2g)

Where: h_f = head loss, f = friction factor, L = length, D = diameter

Machine Design

Safety Factor

SF = σ_yield/σ_working

Where: SF = safety factor, σ_yield = yield strength, σ_working = working stress

Endurance Limit

σ_e = 0.5·σ_uts

Where: σ_e = endurance limit, σ_uts = ultimate tensile strength (for steel)

Gerber Line (Fatigue)

(σ_m/σ_uts)² + (σ_a/σ_e) = 1

Where: σ_m = mean stress, σ_a = alternating stress

Contact Stress (Hertz)

σ_c = √[(F·E)/(π·L·R)]

Where: F = force, E = modulus, L = length, R = radius

Process Selection Guidelines

Selection Criteria

  • Production Volume: High volume favors casting, stamping, injection molding
  • Part Complexity: Complex geometries favor additive manufacturing, investment casting
  • Material: Material properties dictate suitable processes
  • Tolerance Requirements: Precision machining for tight tolerances
  • Surface Finish: Grinding, polishing for fine finishes
  • Cost: Balance tooling costs vs. per-part costs

Maintenance & Reliability

Best practices for equipment maintenance and reliability engineering

Preventive Maintenance

Scheduled maintenance performed to prevent failures

  • Regular inspections
  • Scheduled replacements
  • Calibration and testing
  • Cleaning and lubrication

Benefits:

  • Reduced unplanned downtime
  • Extended equipment life
  • Improved safety
  • Better planning

Predictive Maintenance

Condition-based maintenance using monitoring and analysis

  • Vibration analysis
  • Thermography
  • Oil analysis
  • Ultrasonic testing

Benefits:

  • Optimized maintenance intervals
  • Reduced maintenance costs
  • Early failure detection
  • Data-driven decisions

Reliability Centered Maintenance (RCM)

Systematic approach to determine maintenance requirements

  • Failure mode analysis
  • Criticality assessment
  • Task selection
  • Interval optimization

Benefits:

  • Focused maintenance efforts
  • Improved reliability
  • Cost optimization
  • Risk-based approach

Key Reliability Metrics

MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures)

MTBF = Total Operating Time / Number of Failures

Measures reliability of repairable systems

MTTR (Mean Time To Repair)

MTTR = Total Repair Time / Number of Repairs

Measures maintainability

Availability

Availability = MTBF / (MTBF + MTTR)

Percentage of time system is operational

Reliability

R(t) = e^(-λt)

Probability of survival to time t (λ = failure rate)

Maintenance Checklists

Daily Maintenance

  • Check fluid levels (oil, coolant, hydraulic)
  • Inspect for leaks
  • Check unusual noises or vibrations
  • Verify safety devices are functional
  • Clean work area and equipment

Weekly Maintenance

  • Lubricate moving parts
  • Check belt tension and condition
  • Inspect electrical connections
  • Test safety interlocks
  • Review maintenance logs

Monthly Maintenance

  • Change filters
  • Calibrate instruments
  • Inspect wear components
  • Check alignment
  • Update maintenance records
