# Interactive Diagrams Section

Interactive Engineering Diagrams

Explore mechanical engineering concepts through interactive visualizations

Stress-Strain Curves

Key Points:

  • Yield Point: Where material begins to deform plastically
  • Ultimate Strength: Maximum stress material can withstand
  • Fracture Point: Where material breaks
  • Young's Modulus: Slope of elastic region (stiffness)

Thermodynamic Diagrams

Thermodynamic Processes:

  • Isobaric: Constant pressure process
  • Isothermal: Constant temperature process
  • Isentropic: Constant entropy process
  • Isochoric: Constant volume process

S-N Fatigue Curves

Fatigue Concepts:

  • Endurance Limit: Stress below which material won't fail (steel only)
  • S-N Curve: Stress vs. Number of cycles to failure
  • High Cycle Fatigue: >10^4 cycles, elastic deformation
  • Low Cycle Fatigue: <10^4 cycles, plastic deformation

Creep Curves

Creep Stages:

  • Primary Creep: Decreasing strain rate, work hardening
  • Secondary Creep: Constant strain rate, steady state
  • Tertiary Creep: Increasing strain rate, necking and failure

Heat Transfer Visualization

Heat Transfer Mechanisms:

  • Conduction: Heat transfer through direct contact
  • Convection: Heat transfer through fluid motion
  • Radiation: Heat transfer through electromagnetic waves

Fluid Flow Profiles

Flow Regimes:

  • Laminar (Re < 2300): Smooth, layered flow
  • Transitional (2300 < Re < 4000): Unstable, mixed behavior
  • Turbulent (Re > 4000): Chaotic, mixing flow
